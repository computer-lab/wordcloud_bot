FAQ for computerlab_'s wordcloud functionality

Who is she?
Tweet a message @computerlab_ including the #wordcloud. Additionally, you can include a 'shape' in your message and an additional 'mention' to produce a response with a wordcloud created from your corpus of tweets or another user's tweets. For example:

@henry, look at the facebook shape wordcloud from your tweets that @SbarroChica_bot generated for you! pic.twitter.com/N1QTzLR782

— computer lab (@computerlab_) December 30, 2015

What are the possible shapes?

  • bat
  • bong
  • cat
  • comic sans (we know that's more of a feeling than a shape...)
  • dog taking a poop (include the word "dog" in your tweet for this shape, no need to specify a "pooping dog")
  • facebook guy
  • honk (it is a goose, put 'honk' in your msg tho)
  • horse
  • jumpman jumpman jumpman (don't worry, just have to say it once in your request)
  • kms
  • michael manning color palette (with comic sans, from this painting)
  • "pepe" for the "memesters"
  • penis
  • pope hat
  • sexualized female body (type "woman" in your tweet for this shape)
  • shrek (experimental, hasn't been tested)
  • twitter bird
  • weed leaf

Wait, so how do I do it?
write a tweet like this:

@computerlab_ comic sans #wordcloud

— SbarroChica_bot (@SbarroChica_bot) December 30, 2015

and computerlab_ will respond like this:

@SbarroChica_bot thanks for using @computerlab_ wordcloud generator!, cool tweets and comic sans wordcloud pic.twitter.com/bK0wd37tPk

— computer lab (@computerlab_) December 30, 2015

mention another user in the tweet and we will make a cloud for that user like the one at the top for @henry, it was generated by this request:

@computerlab_ #wordcloud facebook @henry

— SbarroChica_bot (@SbarroChica_bot) December 30, 2015
